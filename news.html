Copper surges past $9,800 as Chile output disappoints and AI-driven demand forecasts revised sharply upward

Copper prices reached their highest level since March 2025 on Friday, driven by a combination of supply-side disappointments from Chile's Escondida mine and a major upward revision to long-term demand forecasts tied to AI data centre buildout and electric vehicle penetration. Goldman Sachs revised its 2027 copper price target to $12,500/t.
